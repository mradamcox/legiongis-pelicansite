Arches

We have been fortunate to become very familiar with Arches, a robust web-based platform designed specifically for cultural heritage inventories. An Arches database allows you to explore and create your records with multiple layers of context. Best of all, to you and your users, it's just a website! Very easy to view, search, and update. To learn more about Arches, visit the official project website, and acquaint yourself with some of the concepts that make the platform unique.

Legion GIS is well-positioned to offer high quality Arches development. Through work with the Getty Conservation Institute, one of the main sponsors of the Arches development, we have already been able to offer a significant amount of support to the growing world-wide Arches community. This has given us the opportunity to be exposed to (and become familiar with) the needs of a wide variety of cultural heritage professionals and organizations. We look forward to sharing this experience with you, and learning more in the meantime!

If you're a curious programmer, you'll be happy to learn that we do all of our Arches package development in public GitHub repositories (see links below). You may also be interested in the Arches forum, or the project repo itself.

Past Arches work from our team

Armed Forces Retirement Home - Washington

see it! | demo | repo

In close collaboration with PRESERVE/scapes, a Washington, DC-based cultural resource preservation firm, Adam worked to migrate the existing AFRH asset database (which used to reside an MS Access) to a custom Arches deployment. Significant customizations were made to the Arches platform, among them the addition of 12 historic map layers, the modification and creation of the database schema to better reflect the AFRH-W assets, significant restructuring of the user-interface, and permission-based report viewing, so not just anyone can view all of the information!

    project in progess, release expected by the end of Summer, 2016

Cane River National Heritage Area, Inc.

see it! | demo | repo

In spring of 2015, Adam worked with the Cane River NHA, located in Natchitoches, Louisiana to create the Cane River Heritage Inventory & Map, which was one of the first independent deployments of then-brand-new Arches v3. The project included the migration of spatial data (a few shapefiles and ESRI geodatabases), tabular data (spreadsheets of cemetery burials), and audio files (CDs containing interviews) into a single Arches database. Now all of this information lives together. In addition to data migration, many historic maps were processed and added as overlays to give the database map a novel and exciting historical dimension.

    official release set for Spring 2016
